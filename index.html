Ground Effect & Modern F1 Cars

Ground effect in F1 is an aerodynamic principle that uses tunnels under the car’s floor to accelerate air, creating a low-pressure area that “sucks” the car to the track, increasing downforce and grip. After being banned in the 1980s due to safety concerns with side skirts, ground effect was reintroduced in the 2022 regulations to reduce dirty air and promote closer racing.
